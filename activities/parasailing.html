Parasailing

50–80 metres up, 10–15 minutes airborne — a bird's-eye view of turquoise waters, white beaches, and emerald islands.

About this experience

A bird's-eye view of the archipelago

Parasailing gives you a bird's-eye view of the Andaman's turquoise waters, white beaches, and lush green islands. Strapped into a harness and attached to a colourful parachute, you're towed behind a speedboat and lifted 50–80 metres into the air for a breathtaking 10–15 minute flight. It's safe, exhilarating, and requires no skill or experience.

Good to know

Where to parasail

Practical info to help you decide if this is right for your trip.

01

Corbyn's Cove, Port Blair

The most accessible parasailing spot — just 7 km from the city centre. Stunning views of the harbour and surrounding islands.

02

Elephant Beach, Havelock

Parasail over the clearest waters in the Andamans with views of the reef below. A premium experience.
