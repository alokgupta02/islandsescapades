12 best places to visit in Andaman

Your complete 2026 island guide — curated from years of taking travellers across the archipelago.

The Andaman Islands are a vast archipelago of over 570 islands in the Bay of Bengal, and choosing where to go can be overwhelming. We've put together this curated list of the 12 absolute must-visit places based on years of taking travellers across these islands.

1. Radhanagar Beach, Havelock

Named Asia's best beach by TIME Magazine, Radhanagar is a sweeping 2 km crescent of white sand backed by a curtain of tropical forest. The sunsets here are legendary — arrive by 4 PM for the best light and stay until the sky turns purple. Swimming is safe and the waters are warm year-round.

2. Cellular Jail, Port Blair

The historic colonial prison where Indian freedom fighters were held during British rule. The evening Light & Sound Show is emotionally powerful and a must-attend. The three-storey structure and its museum tell a moving chapter of India's independence struggle.

3. Elephant Beach, Havelock

A short trek through mangroves or a 15-minute speedboat ride brings you to one of the best snorkelling spots in the Andamans. The coral starts just a few metres offshore, and the shallow waters are calm enough for beginners.

4. Ross Island (Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose Island)

Once the administrative headquarters of the British, Ross Island is now a hauntingly beautiful ruin reclaimed by tropical trees and deer. A short ferry ride from Port Blair, it makes for a fascinating half-day excursion.

5. Neil Island (Shaheed Dweep)

The quieter, more laid-back sister of Havelock. Known for its Natural Rock Bridge formation at Howrah Beach, pristine Bharatpur Beach, and an almost zero-crowds atmosphere. Perfect for cycling and slow travel.

6. North Bay Island

The water sports capital of the Andamans — snorkelling, sea walking, glass-bottom boats, scuba diving, and parasailing all available here. Just 30 minutes from Port Blair by boat.

7. Baratang Island

Home to active mud volcanoes, limestone caves formed over millennia, and dense mangrove-lined waterways. The boat ride through the mangrove creek to reach the caves is itself an experience.

8. Diglipur

The northernmost town in the Andamans, home to Saddle Peak (the highest point at 732 m) and the stunning Ross & Smith twin islands connected by a sand bar. Turtle nesting sites at Kalipur Beach are a draw during nesting season.

9. Chidiya Tapu

Known as the Bird Island, this southern tip of South Andaman is a paradise for birdwatchers and sunset lovers. Spot hornbills, white-bellied sea eagles, and Andaman treepies in the surrounding forest.

10. Jolly Buoy Island

Part of Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park, Jolly Buoy has some of the clearest water in the Andamans. Open only from October to May, it's a premier spot for snorkelling and glass-bottom boat rides.

11. Long Island

For travellers seeking solitude, Long Island offers pristine Lalaji Bay — accessible only by boat or a 45-minute trek. No crowds, no commercial activity, just pure untouched beach.

12. Rangat

A hidden gem with Amkunj Beach (eco-friendly turtle nesting site), Cuthbert Bay (wildlife sanctuary), and Yerrata mangrove boardwalk. Barely touched by mass tourism, Rangat is the real, unhurried Andaman.
