Before you fly

Complete packing guide for Andaman

Everything you need (and don't need) to bring — distilled from years of sending travellers to the islands.

Packing for the Andamans is straightforward — the climate is tropical, the vibe is relaxed, and you'll spend most of your time in beachwear. But there are a few essentials you absolutely shouldn't forget. Here's our complete guide based on years of sending travellers to these islands.

Documents (Critical)

Carry a valid government photo ID (Aadhaar card or passport). Foreign nationals need a Restricted Area Permit (RAP), which is issued on arrival at Port Blair airport. Keep printed copies of ferry bookings, hotel reservations, and return flight tickets. A waterproof document pouch is worth the small investment.

Clothing

Light, breathable fabrics are essential — cotton and linen work best. Pack 3–4 t-shirts, 2 pairs of shorts, a light dress or two, swimwear (at least 2 sets so one is always dry), a light long-sleeve shirt for sun protection, and a rain jacket or poncho (especially if visiting during shoulder season). Avoid heavy jeans and dark colours that absorb heat.

Footwear

Waterproof sandals or flip-flops for the beach, a pair of sturdy walking shoes for treks (Elephant Beach trail, Saddle Peak), and water shoes if you plan to walk on rocky beaches or snorkel from shore.

Toiletries & Sun Protection

Reef-safe sunscreen (SPF 50+) is non-negotiable — the tropical sun is fierce. Bring insect repellent (mosquitoes are active at dusk), aloe vera gel for after-sun care, a good moisturiser, and basic toiletries. Availability on smaller islands is limited and prices are marked up.

Medicines

Carry seasickness tablets (Avomine/Meclizine) — ferry rides can be rough. A basic first-aid kit with antiseptic cream, band-aids, antihistamines, paracetamol, and any prescription medication you need. Pharmacies exist in Port Blair and Havelock but not on smaller islands.

Gear & Gadgets

A dry bag (for boat rides and water activities), a power bank (10,000+ mAh — electricity can be unreliable on remote islands), an underwater camera or waterproof phone case, a reusable water bottle, and binoculars if you're into birdwatching. Cash is important — ATMs exist only in Port Blair, Havelock, and Neil. Carry enough for your entire trip.

What NOT to Pack

Leave single-use plastics behind (banned in the Andamans), heavy winter clothing, expensive jewellery, large suitcases (backpacks are easier for ferries and autos), and more than 2 pairs of shoes.
