# ADR 0001 — Use a place-agnostic evocative name (*Islands Escapades*)

**Status:** Accepted (supersedes an earlier draft that proposed a descriptive name)
**Date:** 2026-05-28

## Context

The agency sells Andaman & Nicobar trips to a broad audience anchored in domestic Indian honeymooners and families. Brand personality is *quietly confident and place-led*.

Two naming territories were considered:

- **Descriptive** (e.g., *Andaman Voyages*) — strong cold-search SEO, immediate trust, weak memorability, weak trademark, sounds like every competitor.
- **Evocative** (e.g., *Saltwater*, *Islands Escapades*) — weaker immediate trust, stronger memorability and trademark, distinctive in a crowded "Andaman + noun" landscape, available domains, broader brand stretch.

Initial inclination favored descriptive for sales velocity. That was overridden by three practical realities:

1. The "Andaman + [noun]" namespace is saturated — *Andaman Tours*, *Andaman Holidays*, *Andaman Voyages*, *Andaman Travels*, *Andaman Trails*, *Andaman Journeys* all exist or are taken.
2. Matching domains are largely unavailable or premium-priced.
3. Personality-fit: "quietly confident, place-led" reads as boutique. Descriptive names read transactional. Mismatch.

## Decision

The brand is named **Islands Escapades**. The word *Andaman* does not appear in the name and is carried instead at the metadata, content, and lockup level (e.g., `<title>Islands Escapades — Andaman Travel & Honeymoon Packages</title>`, hero copy, page H1s, alt text).

## Consequences

- **Visual identity must do place work the name does not.** Photography, palette, and typographic treatment have to telegraph Andaman immediately, because the wordmark does not. This is a higher bar for the visual system than a descriptive name would have required.
- **SEO is fought at the content/title-tag level, not at the brand-name level.** Acceptable; standard SEO practice handles this. Costs some click-through on cold "Andaman travel agency" searches where competitor names contain the keyword and ours does not.
- **Word-of-mouth requires an anchor.** Customers referring the brand will often append "—it's an Andaman company." Sales/marketing copy templates should make the Andaman association unmissable in the first paragraph of every customer-facing surface.
- **The "Escapades" connotation risk is accepted.** The word has a secondary cheeky/romantic-liaison meaning in casual usage. Visual identity and copy tone must lean *quietly confident* (per brand personality) to neutralize it; loud or playful treatments would amplify the unwanted reading.
- **Brand-build runway is now load-bearing.** Year-1 revenue will lean harder on paid acquisition and content/SEO than a descriptive name would have. Plan accordingly.
- **The "second evocative brand for luxury/international" plan is now redundant in spirit** — this *is* the evocative brand. If a second brand is launched later, it should differentiate on positioning (e.g., explicit luxury/concierge), not on naming territory.

## Alternatives considered

- **Descriptive (*Andaman Voyages*, etc.)** — rejected per the saturation, domain, and personality-mismatch reasons above.
- **Hybrid (*Andaman Saltwater*, *Andaman Islands Escapades*)** — rejected as a length-tax and a compromise of both directions.
- **Stronger evocative alternatives (*Saltwater*, *Tideline*, *Coral Routes*, *Pelagic*)** — surfaced and offered. *Islands Escapades* was chosen with full awareness that *Escapades* is less editorially strong than these alternatives.
