Frequently asked

Travel questions, answered

Everything you need to know before your Andaman trip — permits, ferries, safety, packing and more.

Do I need a permit to visit the Andaman Islands?
Indian nationals do not need a special permit for most tourist areas (Port Blair, Havelock, Neil). Foreign nationals receive a Restricted Area Permit (RAP) on arrival at Port Blair airport, valid for 30 days. Some remote areas like tribal reserves require additional permits from the Deputy Commissioner's office.
How do I reach the Andaman Islands?
By air: Direct flights operate from Chennai, Kolkata, Delhi, Bangalore, and Hyderabad to Veer Savarkar International Airport in Port Blair. Flight time from Chennai/Kolkata is approximately 2–2.5 hours. By sea: Government ships sail from Chennai and Kolkata (3–4 days), but flights are recommended for most travellers.
What is the best time to visit Andaman?
October to May is the best season, with clear skies, calm seas, and pleasant temperatures (24–34°C). Peak tourist season is December to February. The monsoon (June–September) brings heavy rainfall and rough seas; many water activities and ferries may be suspended.
How do I travel between islands?
Government ferries and private cruises (Makruzz, Nautika, Green Ocean) connect Port Blair to Havelock and Neil Island. Journey times range from 90 minutes to 2.5 hours. Book ferry tickets in advance during peak season. For remote islands (Baratang, Diglipur), government ferries and road + ferry combos are available.
Is the Andaman safe for solo travellers and women?
Yes, the Andaman Islands are considered one of the safest destinations in India. The local population is friendly, crime rates are very low, and tourist infrastructure is well-developed on the main islands. Standard travel precautions apply as with any destination.
Do I need to know swimming for water activities?
Not necessarily. Sea walking requires no swimming at all. Snorkelling is done with life jackets. Discover Scuba diving is guided by certified instructors in shallow waters. Only advanced scuba diving and deep-water activities require swimming proficiency.
Are there ATMs and mobile network coverage?
ATMs are available in Port Blair, Havelock, and Neil Island (SBI, Axis Bank, ICICI). Carry sufficient cash for remote islands. BSNL has the best network coverage. Airtel and Jio work in Port Blair and parts of Havelock but are unreliable elsewhere. Wi-Fi is available at most hotels but speeds are slow.
What should I pack for an Andaman trip?
Light cotton clothing, swimwear, reef-safe sunscreen (SPF 50+), insect repellent, seasickness tablets, waterproof phone case, power bank, cash, a dry bag, sturdy sandals, and water shoes. Check our complete packing guide for a detailed list.
Can I customise a package?
Absolutely! All our packages can be customised based on your budget, interests, travel dates, and group size. Contact us via WhatsApp or the contact form and our trip planners will create a bespoke itinerary for you within 24 hours.
What is the cancellation and refund policy?
Cancellations made 30+ days before departure receive a full refund minus processing fees. 15–30 days: 50% refund. Less than 15 days: no refund. Full details are available on our cancellation policy page.
Are meals included in the packages?
Most packages include breakfast. Some premium and luxury packages include all meals. Meal plans can be added to any package during customisation. The Andamans offer excellent seafood and local cuisine at very reasonable prices if you prefer to eat out.
Is Andaman suitable for elderly travellers?
Yes, with appropriate planning. Port Blair, Havelock, and Neil Island have good roads and accessible beaches. Sea walking and glass-bottom boats are gentle activities suitable for older travellers. We recommend avoiding strenuous treks and long ferry rides for those with mobility concerns. Our team can plan a comfortable, senior-friendly itinerary.
